About Me

I am an Electrical Engineering student with a deep interest in combining technical knowledge and creativity to solve real-world problems. Over the course of my studies, I have gained experience in multiple programming languages, including Assembly, C, C++, and Python, which I have applied to a variety of engineering projects ranging from microcontroller programming to automation systems. In addition, I have developed skills in 3D design, allowing me to design and prototype custom mechanical components that complement my electrical designs.
One of the projects I am most proud of is my work on an Automatic Color Sorting Conveyor Belt, where I integrated coding, circuitry, and 3D modeling to build a functional machine. This experience allowed me to explore hardware-software interaction, troubleshoot complex issues, and refine my problem-solving abilities.
Beyond technical growth, I am also driven by the impact engineering can have on people’s lives. For example, in my ALS project, I focused on developing solutions that support individuals living with neuromuscular conditions. This project inspired me to use my engineering skills not only to innovate but also to contribute to my community in meaningful ways.
Alongside my coursework and projects, I tutor mathematics and science subjects. Tutoring has strengthened my ability to break down complex concepts into clear, understandable steps, and it has reinforced my passion for education and mentorship. I believe that the combination of technical expertise, creativity, and the ability to communicate effectively is essential for any engineer.
Looking forward, I am eager to continue expanding my knowledge, pursuing projects that integrate electronics, programming, and design, and using my skills to make a positive difference both within the engineering field and in the broader community.

Projects

Automatic Color Sorting Conveyor Belt

Developed an automatic color sorting conveyor belt using Arduino, color sensors, and 3D printed components to sort objects based on color.

Assistive Device for ALS Patients

Designed and prototyped an assistive device to help ALS patients be able to move their arms up and down using a linear actuator and a mechanical scissor lifting design that can hold +20 pounds.

Real-Time Voice Captioning and Translation System

Designed and programmed in Python an C in the PIC16F1829 to create a system where you can pick any two languages to translate between. As you talk into the system it automaticlly translates and puts up LCD captions of the translation.

Rasberry-Pi Battle Bot

Worked with a team of four to design and build a Raspberry Pi–powered battle bot featuring a forklift-inspired robotic arm, defensive shield, and laser blaster, all controlled via Bluetooth. Applied 3D design and Python programming skills to create functional components within strict competition size limits.
